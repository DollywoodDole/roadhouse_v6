# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Saskatchewan and Prairie-region business operators — automotive dealers, agricultural operators, trades businesses, hospitality operators, retailers, and professional service firms. They are builders and owners, not marketers or design enthusiasts. They land on this site when they have a real problem (no platform, weak brand, no acquisition infrastructure) and they are evaluating whether RoadHouse Studio can handle it. They are skeptical of agencies, price-sensitive to surprises, and time-poor. They need to see proof before they hear pitch.

## Product Purpose

RoadHouse Studio is the creative and build arm of the RoadHouse ecosystem — it does client work (web platforms, brand identity, growth infrastructure) and builds house IP (broadcast, content, owned formats). The site's job is to convert serious operators into project leads. It does this by demonstrating capability through live work (the Motors platform is the case study), by being direct about pricing and process, and by projecting the confidence of a team that ships real infrastructure — not a team that talks about creativity.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Operational. Proven.

Voice: short sentences, no filler, no hype. Everything stated is something that can be pointed at. The site speaks like an operator, not an agency. It does not "empower" or "elevate" — it builds and ships.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel they are dealing with people who have already solved the problem they are bringing, not people who are excited to try.

## Anti-references

- Generic Webflow agency templates — the site should not have a hero with a tagline + CTA button + client logos strip
- Dark SaaS dashboards (Stripe, Linear, Vercel aesthetic) — no frosted glass cards, no gradient blobs, no rounded-card grids
- "Passionate creative team" agency sites — no team photos with arms crossed, no "we believe design can change the world" copy, no 5-word mission statement in huge type
- Page-builder aesthetics (Elementor, Divi, Squarespace energy) — no stacked full-width alternating sections, no icon grids, no accordion FAQs
- Standard Next.js dark starter — no purple/blue accent, no code snippet decoration, no developer-portfolio feel

The site should feel like an operation, not a portfolio. It should feel like the same people who built the live infrastructure also built this page.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the live work, don't describe it.** The Motors platform is the proof. Put it in frame. Proof beats claims.
2. **Every price, timeline, and process step is a trust signal.** Fixed scope, 7–21 days, full ownership — these are differentiators that belong in the hierarchy, not the footnotes.
3. **Earned confidence.** No hype. No urgency tactics. The site speaks at a register that assumes the visitor is competent; it does not sell, it qualifies.
4. **The design proves the claim.** If the site claims to build excellent platforms, the site must itself be excellent — technically precise, visually intentional, and responsive. Shoddy implementation contradicts the pitch.
5. **Operator language, not agency language.** "Build", "ship", "own" — not "craft", "journey", "transform". The copy is written for people who run operations, not people who consume content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA — standard floor. All body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Interactive elements ≥ 3:1. Reduced motion alternatives for all GSAP and CSS animations. Keyboard navigable. No information conveyed by color alone.
