## The Unified Thesis

The DeSci layer and the physical compound are the same thing described at two different levels of abstraction. This document resolves that split.

**The compound IS the DeSci lab.**

> RoadHouse is a physical research community that runs applied experiments in human performance, economics, and technology. The compound is the laboratory. The guild economy is the coordination experiment. The community members are the participants. The data generated is the research output.

This single sentence satisfies four audiences simultaneously:
- A **Mitacs reviewer** sees an applied research partnership with a physical site and defined methodology
- A **SaskInnovates officer** sees regional infrastructure investment with measurable community impact
- A **Praetor-tier sponsor** sees a flagship venue with national storytelling potential
- A **prospective member** sees a place worth upgrading their membership to access

## What the Compound Is

The RoadHouse Compound is a permanently operational physical node — a live-in, work-in environment that collapses the distinctions between work and life, health and money, creativity and structure, community and output.

It is not a retreat. Not a coworking space. Not an event venue alone.

It is a **generator** — an environment designed so that natural human activity (working, training, creating, socialising) simultaneously produces business value through content, community, and coordinated output.

### Core Functions

| Function | Description |
|---|---|
| **Living laboratory** | Applied research on human performance, coordination, and community economics — conducted through documented daily protocols |
| **Content production environment** | Physical space engineered for streaming and long-form content creation — compound life IS the content |
| **Community anchor** | The place Steward and Praetor members have access to — the physical manifestation of membership value |
| **Event infrastructure** | Venue for RoadHouse gatherings, member summits, and public-facing events that generate both revenue and content |
| **Venture base** | Operations centre for the Venture Guild — deal review, partner meetings, due diligence sessions |

## Flagship Node — Saskatchewan

The first and defining RoadHouse node is in rural Saskatchewan. This is not a compromise — it is a structural advantage.

### Why Saskatchewan

| Factor | Advantage |
|---|---|
| **Tax jurisdiction** | Combined 10–11% effective corporate rate on first $500k active income — structurally lower than every major Canadian province and most US states |
| **Land cost** | Rural Saskatchewan land acquisition is a fraction of comparable property in BC, Alberta, or Ontario |
| **Grant ecosystem** | SaskInnovates, SaskCulture, Saskatchewan Film Employment Tax Credit, and proximity to University of Saskatchewan Mitacs partnerships |
| **Narrative** | Saskatchewan independence and frontier identity maps directly to the RoadHouse brand — this is not incidental, it is the story |
| **Space** | Physical scale available in rural Saskatchewan is impossible in urban centres — the compound can be built to specification, not constrained to available commercial real estate |

### Timeline — Flagship Node

| Phase | Milestone | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Current** | Mobile operations — annual tour from Saskatoon to West Coast | 2026 |
| **Year 2** | Land identified and optioned in rural Saskatchewan | Q3–Q4 2026 |
| **Year 3** | Land acquired, initial infrastructure built, first on-site events | 2027–2028 |
| **Year 4–5** | Compound fully operational as hospitality and event venue; permanent residency for Praetor-tier members | 2028–2030 |

## The Mobile-First Bridge

Until the physical compound exists, the annual tour IS the compound — mobile, temporary, but operationally equivalent in function.

The tour runs from Saskatoon to the West Coast. Each stop is a temporary node activation:
- Content production on location
- Community member meetups and activations
- Sponsor and partner activations
- Guild contribution events (Frontier Guild operational lead)

The tour generates the content, the community proof, and the revenue that funds the permanent compound build. It is not a compromise for the interim — it is the content strategy.

## Node Scaling Model

The flagship Saskatchewan compound is Node 1. The long-term architecture is a distributed network of RoadHouse nodes — each a licensed, branded compound environment operating under the RoadHouse model.

### Node Tiers

| Tier | Description | Licensing Model |
|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | Saskatchewan — founder-owned, fully integrated | Not licensed — owned and operated by Praetorian Holdings |
| **Partner Node** | Licensed to aligned operators in other markets | Revenue share + brand licence fee + content integration requirements |
| **Pop-up Node** | Temporary activations (tour stops, events) | Event-by-event agreement; no ongoing licence |

### Node Licensing Revenue (Year 4–5)

Partner nodes generate three revenue streams for Praetorian Holdings:
1. **Brand licence fee** — annual flat fee for use of RoadHouse brand and operational playbook
2. **Revenue share** — percentage of node revenue in excess of operating costs
3. **Content integration** — node operators contribute content to the RoadHouse media library under a content licence agreement

## The DeSci Research Layer

Every compound protocol is documented. Every protocol generates data. The data is the research.

### Active Research Domains

| Domain | Protocol | Research Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Human Performance** | 30-day physical training + sleep + nutrition tracking | What does a structured environment do to measurable performance markers? |
| **Community Economics** | Guild contribution tracking + $ROAD accrual + tier advancement | What reward structures produce sustained community labour? What is the elasticity of contribution to reward size? |
| **Digital Coordination** | DAO governance participation + proposal submission rates + voting behaviour | How do distributed communities make decisions? What governance structures produce alignment? |
| **Creative Output** | Content volume + audience engagement + member-generated vs. founder-generated content | What environmental and incentive structures maximise creative output? |

### The DeSci Protocol Sprint (30 Days)

The DeSci Protocol Sprint is a structured 30-day participation programme — members commit to a defined protocol (training, contribution, governance participation) and log their participation daily.

**Why this matters beyond research:**
- Completing a full DeSci protocol sprint is one of the four Founding NFT qualification conditions (see `membership-model.md`)
- It creates a cohort of deeply invested members who have committed 30 days to the community before receiving the credential
- The protocol completion rate is the primary Mitacs research data point — measurable, documentable, publishable

### Grant Narratives

**For Mitacs Accelerate:**
> "RoadHouse operates a physical research environment conducting applied experiments in community coordination, human performance, and digital governance. The compound and its protocols generate longitudinal data on participant behaviour, governance decision-making, and the relationship between physical community infrastructure and digital coordination outcomes. University of Saskatchewan partnership would formalise the research methodology and enable peer-reviewed publication of findings."

**For SaskInnovates:**
> "The RoadHouse Compound is Saskatchewan-based community infrastructure with regional economic impact — land acquisition, employment, events, and tourism — anchored by digital platform technology developed under IRAP and SR&ED support. The node model scales Saskatchewan's innovation profile nationally through licensed operations in other markets."

**For SR&ED:**
> "RoadHouse's contribution tracking, $ROAD accrual, and governance infrastructure constitute qualifying SR&ED expenditures — novel technical work on decentralised coordination systems with no prior art in the cooperative/DAO hybrid model being developed."

## Compound Access by Membership Tier

| Tier | Compound Access |
|---|---|
| Guest | No access |
| Regular | No access |
| Ranch Hand | Public events only |
| Partner | Public events + partner-tier gatherings |
| Steward | Full access during events + working visits by arrangement |
| Praetor | Permanent compound residency privileges |

The Steward tier is the compound access unlock. The 10,000 $ROAD requirement for Steward tier is the threshold at which a member has demonstrated sufficient contribution to the community to warrant physical access to the infrastructure their labour helped build. This framing — access earned through contribution, not purchased — is both the membership design principle and the securities positioning principle.

## Interest Registration (Pre-Compound)

Before the physical compound exists, interest registration serves three functions:
1. **Pipeline** — identifies Steward and Praetor tier members who intend to use compound access when available
2. **Grant evidence** — a waitlist of qualified members demonstrates demand to SaskInnovates and potential institutional partners
3. **Sponsor proposition** — a defined addressable audience of high-tier members is the foundation of the Praetor-level sponsorship package

Interest registration lives at `/compound` — the page that consolidates this document's content into a member-facing experience.
